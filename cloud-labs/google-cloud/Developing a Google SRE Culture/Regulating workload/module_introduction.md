# Regulating Workload in Site Reliability Engineering (SRE)

The final step in the **Site Reliability Engineering (SRE) journey** is **regulating workload**.

This stage focuses on ensuring that engineering teams maintain a **sustainable balance between operational responsibilities and engineering work**.

---

# What This Module Covers

In this section, you will learn about several important SRE practices, including:

- **Measuring everything**
- **Tracking reliability**
- **Measuring and managing toil**
- **Understanding the role of monitoring**

Measurement plays a central role in ensuring that systems remain **reliable, scalable, and efficient**.

---

# Cultural Foundations of Measurement

This module also introduces important **organizational culture principles**, including:

- **Goal setting**
- **Transparency**
- **Data-driven decision making**

These cultural practices help organizations maintain clarity and alignment across engineering teams.

---

# Connection to DevOps Principles

Many of the ideas discussed in this module overlap with the **core pillars of DevOps**.

Measurement is especially important because it supports **all SRE practices**, helping teams evaluate performance, identify issues, and continuously improve their systems.

---

# Key Takeaway

Measurement acts as the **foundation that connects all SRE practices**.

By measuring reliability, workload, and operational performance, teams can make informed decisions that improve both **system stability and engineering productivity**.
